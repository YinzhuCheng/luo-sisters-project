Three Lines

Three Story Lines

The surface is the tea party, the middle is the sister relationship, and the deeper layer is anxiety and choice in the AIGC era.

Surface Line

The sisters prepare a small Alice-flavored tea party: venue, outfits, invitations, photos, sweets, entry route, and decorations.

Emotional Line

Qingyou wants the dream to land safely; Arisu wants the door to keep glowing.

Era Line

AIGC gradually appears as tool, companion, adviser, and recommender. It helps the characters and amplifies their coping habits.

AIGC Rhythm

AIGC Appearance Rhythm

AIGC should not steal the visible story; it becomes an anxiety amplifier in the middle and late phases.

Early | 5%

Background air: posters, references, schedules.

Early-Mid | 15%

Efficiency contrast: Qingyou feels a small sting.

Middle | 35%

Side characters show companion, adviser, and generator uses.

Mid-Late | 45%

AIGC directly amplifies the sisters' conflict.

Late | 20%

The tool returns to the background while real experience closes the theme.

Cast Function

Supporting Cast And AI Use Styles

Side characters carry companionship, advice, and system-entry dimensions.

Nanxing | AI As Companion

A long-term chat companion gives stable memory and comfort. Her growth is toward slight risk in real relationships.

Mingxia | AI As Adviser

A student-council type who asks systems to rate risk, traffic, and routes. She represents the temptation of optimal answers.

White Rabbit System | Era Entry

A bundle of recommendation, generation, reminders, and guidance. Useful, cheerful, and always a little too urgent.

Scene Hooks

Key Scene Hooks

Reusable memory points for chapters, key visuals, and future web displays.

  • Arisu writes an invitation asking guests to bring one piece of getting lost before the tea cools.
  • An LLM makes the invitation clearer and more formal; Arisu says it closed her door.
  • Arisu tells Qingyou that she even scheduled getting lost.
  • Qingyou admits she is afraid of becoming unnecessary if everything she is good at can be replaced.
  • The sisters rewrite prompts together: preserve the feeling of getting lost while ensuring guests can return safely.
  • Rain, puddles, umbrellas, wrong turns, and laughter become the real memory.
